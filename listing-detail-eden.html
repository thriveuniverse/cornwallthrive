About The Eden Project

The Eden Project is a popular visitor attraction in Cornwall, England. Inside the two biomes are plants that are collected from many diverse climates and environments. The project is located in a reclaimed china clay pit, located 2 km (1.2 mi) from the town of St Blazey and 5 km (3 mi) from the larger town of St Austell.

The complex is dominated by two huge enclosures consisting of adjoining domes that house thousands of plant species, and each enclosure emulates a natural biome. The biomes consist of hundreds of hexagonal and pentagonal, inflated, plastic cells supported by steel frames. The largest of the two biomes simulates a rainforest environment and the second, a Mediterranean environment. The attraction also has an outside botanical garden which is home to many plants and wildlife native to Cornwall and the UK in general.

The project was conceived by Tim Smit and designed by architect Nicholas Grimshaw and engineering firm Anthony Hunt and Associates (now part of Sinclair Knight Merz). Davis Langdon carried out the project management, Sir Robert McAlpine and Alfred McAlpine did the construction, MERO designed and built the biomes, and Arup was the services engineer, economic consultant, environmental engineer and transportation engineer. Land Use Consultants led the masterplan and landscape design. The project took 2½ years to construct and opened to the public on 17 March 2001.

Highlights

  • Rainforest Biome - the largest indoor rainforest in the world
  • Mediterranean Biome - featuring plants from Mediterranean climates
  • Outdoor Gardens - showcasing 20 acres of plants and landscapes
  • The Core - education center with interactive exhibits
  • Seasonal events and festivals throughout the year
  • England's longest and fastest zip wire
  • Giant Bee sculpture and pollination exhibits
  • Multiple restaurants and cafés serving locally-sourced food
  • Gift shops with eco-friendly products
  • Regular concerts and performances in the summer

Sarah Johnson

Visited April 2025

"A truly magical experience for the whole family"

We visited the Eden Project during our family holiday in Cornwall and were absolutely blown away. The biomes are incredible, especially the Rainforest one where you can experience different climate zones as you climb to the top. The kids loved the canopy walkway and learning about the plants.

The outdoor gardens were beautiful too, and there were plenty of activities to keep everyone entertained. The food options were excellent with lots of locally-sourced produce. Definitely worth the admission price for a full day out!

David Thompson

Visited March 2025

"Impressive but expensive"

The Eden Project is undoubtedly an impressive feat of engineering and botany. The biomes are spectacular and contain an amazing variety of plants from around the world. The educational aspects are well done and informative.

My only criticism is the price - it's quite expensive for a family day out, especially if you add in food and souvenirs. I'd recommend booking online in advance for a discount and planning to spend the whole day there to get your money's worth.

Emma Wilson

Visited February 2025

"Perfect even in winter!"

I wasn't sure if visiting in February would be worthwhile, but it was actually the perfect time to go! The biomes provide a warm tropical escape from the winter weather, and there were far fewer crowds than in summer.

The rainforest biome was particularly impressive - hot and humid with amazing plants and waterfalls. The Mediterranean biome was more comfortable temperature-wise and had beautiful scents from the herbs and flowers. Staff were knowledgeable and friendly. Highly recommend!

By Car

The Eden Project is well signposted from the A30 from Exeter and the A391/390 from St Austell. There is ample parking on site (parking fee applies).

By Public Transport

The nearest railway station is St Austell. From there, you can take the dedicated Eden Project bus service or a local taxi.

Nearby Towns

  • St Austell - 5 miles (8 km)
  • Fowey - 7 miles (11 km)

  • Mevagissey - 10 miles (16 km)
  • Truro - 20 miles (32 km)

Parking

Parking costs £8 per car. Free for Eden Project Members and Annual Pass holders. Accessible parking spaces are available closer to the entrance.
